Service design 101

Service design is about making your service more useful, usable, desirable, and efficient. We consider all possible interactions between customers and your service. Human, physical, and digital. We ask: how might we enhance your customer’s end-to-end experience? Then we turn our thinking towards your operations. We ask: how can we optimise how people, processes, and technologies work together to deliver your service?

More often than not, this leads us to get out of the office and talk to the public about using your service. Experience research fosters a deeper understanding of your customer’s needs and their challenges. Spot opportunities to add service value and to reduce your cost to serve.

Armed with user insights and a better understanding of your challenges, together we will re-imagine your service and explore new approaches to delivery. We use a co-design approach that empowers service delivery teams to explore new service solutions, prototype their ideas, and test these out with customers.

Design thinking

We follow a design led approach that focuses on unveiling actionable insights, and creating new service solutions.

When the first diamond begins to open, we open our mind to exploring the assumed problem. When the diamond begins to close back down, we start to make sense of our insights to pinpoint the real problem.

When we enter the second diamond we start to focus on finding a solution to the problem(s) identified. But, it is only after brainstorming all possible solutions, that we begin to focus on defining and refining any one solution.

This deliberate convergent and divergent thinking is known as ‘design thinking’.
